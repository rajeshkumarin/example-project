Nasal Polyp Removal

Nasal polyps are polypoidal masses arising mainly from the mucous membranes of the nose and paranasal sinuses lining the inside of the large air spaces that exist in the bones of the face (the sinuses) or of the nose.

Nasal polyps are usually classified into antrochoanal polyps and ethmoidal polyps. Antrochoanal polyps arise from the maxillary sinuses and are much less common; an ethmoidal polyp arises from the ethmoidal sinuses. Antrochoanal polyps are usually single and unilateral whereas ethmoidal polyps are multiple and bilateral. Sometimes polyps are caused by allergy or infection.

The polyps in the nose obstruct in smooth breathing. These sometimes block the sinuses and cause sinus infections. In certain cases it blocks the gland which gives the sense of smell. The chances of a sinus getting infected are reduced and one is able to breathe through the nose more comfortably after removing the polyps.

Cause

Allergy, Chronic sinusitis and Chronic inflammation of indeterminate etiology are the main cause of nasal polyps.
BRIEF ABOUT THE PROCEDURE

The polyps are removed using endoscopic surgery and the procedure takes approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour.

The procedure is done under general anesthesia and you will be completely asleep. The operation will be carried out inside the nose and hence there will be no visible cuts on the outside and no change in shape of the nose. Small instruments will be used inside the nose to take out each polyp which is then sent to the laboratory to be examined under a microscope. 

The surgeon will wash out your sinuses to remove any infection that may be trapped inside by the polyps. If there is any bleeding, the surgeon will put cotton gauze (a pack) soaked in Vaseline inside your nose to stop the bleeding, or he may use a dressing like sponge rubber. Sometimes if the nose is very narrow inside, the surgeon may leave plastic splints inside the nose to stop the tissues sticking together when they heal up. Because you are asleep you will not feel any pain at all during the operation. You will probably be fit to go home the day after your operation.
TIPS ON RECOVERY

Recovery from this type of surgery is anywhere from one to three weeks. Pre-post surgery, sinus rinses with warm water mixed with a small amount sodium chloride & sodium bicarbonate can be very helpful to clear the sinuses. This method can be also used as a preventative measure to discourage the polyps from growing back and should be used in combination with a nasal steroid.
